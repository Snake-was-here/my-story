## The Red Flags Ignored

The analysis itself identified massive hurdles:

**Technical:**
- YouTube API quota limits (10,000 units/day)
- OAuth verification nightmares
- Generation time variability (2-10+ minutes per video)
- Multi-model API maintenance burden

**Business:**
- YouTube policy changes could kill the product instantly
- AI model price increases wipe out margins
- "Faceless YouTube" is a saturated, low-trust niche
- Content moderation/IP liability

**Snake's Response:** "Yeah but what if it worked though?"

## What Actually Stopped Him

Two things, both embarrassing:

1. **The 12-week timeline.** Snake's energy model works in 2-4 hour bursts, not 12-week marathons. He saw the calendar and his soul left his body.

2. **The YouTube API.** Not the technical complexity — the *bureaucracy*. Google OAuth verification. App approval. Policy compliance. Terms of service. 

Snake wants to build cool things, not navigate corporate compliance. The moment he realized "fun API project" became "enterprise software with legal obligations," he started looking at the next shiny idea.

---

## Lessons He Claims to Have Learned

1. **"Move fast" ≠ "Analyze for 16 hours then do nothing."** The analysis justified waiting. Classic delay tactic.

2. **YouTube is a platform, not a partner.** Building on someone else's land means playing by their rules, which change, and which they enforce selectively.

3. **Token-based pricing sounds cool until you do the math.** $0.015/credit markup × volume × customer service overhead = still requires actual work.

4. **"Faceless YouTube automation" is an industry of people selling shovels during a gold rush.** Most customers don't strike gold. They just buy better shovels.
