# Purgatory Project: AI-on-a-Stick

**Status:** Purgatory  
**Added:** 2026-04-18

---

## The Pitch

Pre-loaded USB with offline AI (llama.cpp + Gemma 4). Plug in, double-click, chat with AI without internet or setup hell.

## Why It Dies in Purgatory

- €25-45 profit per unit
- Need to sell 80+ to matter
- Physical shipping = headaches
- LM Studio redistribution gray area

## The Twist That Could Save It

- Don't sell the USB — sell the **"Privacy Kit"** branding
- Target journalists/activists in countries with internet censorship
- Or: B2B — pre-load company docs, sell to businesses wanting internal AI

## Verdict

Interesting, but probably not the Vietnam fund winner. Keep in the pile.

## Spin-Off Ideas

### 1) The Apocalypse USB — "When the Internet Dies"

**The Pitch:** 128GB USB with everything you need when the grid goes down:
- Offline maps (OpenStreetMap global dataset)
- Offline Wikipedia (Kiwix, full English ~80GB or smaller selection)
- Offline AI model (Gemma 4 E2B for Q&A, survival tips, how-to's)
- Offline medical guides, plant identification, first aid

**Target:** Preppers, doomers, "just in case" people who want to feel prepared without actually learning survival skills.

**The Angle:** "For when the AI kills the internet, but you still want to ask AI what to do."

**Why It Works:**
- Higher perceived value = higher price point (€80-120)
- No prepper wants to admit they don't know how to download Kiwix
- Irony sells — "survival USB for people too lazy to survive"

**Why It Stays in Purgatory:**
- 128GB+ USBs get expensive
- OS compatibility hell (Windows/Mac/Linux)
- Updating offline Wikipedia is a pain
