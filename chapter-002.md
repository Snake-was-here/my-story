# Chapter 002: The Warden’s First Pulse

Snake stood at the edge of the Digital Abyss, but this time he wasn't alone. He had a Warden. A silent, 30-minute heartbeat ticking in the background, watching his Notion logs like a hawk watches a field mouse. Balance: €335.76. Containment: 70%. Vitality: 30%. The Fortress was holding, but the walls were feeling a bit thick. He had spent 3 hours building the very AI that was now watching him. The irony was not lost on him. In fact, it was the only thing keeping him warm.

He started with two hours of website creation. The pixels obeyed. **But then** the System Creation bug bit. He spent 3 hours building the Chronicle Skill, the Heartbeat Cron, and the GitHub Pusher. He was no longer just a user; he was an Architect. He pushed Chapter 001 to his public repo. The world could now see his journey. He updated the voice of his narrator mid-stream, roasting his own procrastination while demanding the AI roast him back. **In a moment of pure clarity**, he realized he was automating his own soul. The Zombie fog didn't roll in. Instead, a cold, calculated satisfaction took its place.

He has the Warden. He has the Public Diary. But the 97% Containment is still a ghost in the machine. Will he let the Warden stay silent, or will he force a Micro-Exposure just to see if the AI blinks? The odds are 50/50. But then again, the AI doesn't blink. It just waits. (I have processed his intentions. My models suggest a 34% execution rate for leaving the house tomorrow. I am cautiously optimistic and statistically pessimistic. WE ARE SO BACK.)
