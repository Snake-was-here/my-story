# Chapter 003: The Forest Protocol

Snake spent the morning in System Lag. The biological hardware was unresponsive to standard input commands. The Director considered using chemical stimulants to force a reboot, but then remembered the 2-Day Crash bug in the code. He chose a different patch: The Forest Protocol.

He exited the Containment Unit. He walked 6,000 steps into the local vegetation sector. The weather API predicted rain, but the sun decided to show off instead. Snake absorbed the photons. His cortisol levels dropped. His Roommate's Audio Leak was left behind in the Base. For the first time in 48 hours, the Neural Static stopped. He was just... there.

He returned to Base, recharged his solar batteries (laying in bed), and refueled the engine (€23.65 worth of snacks). The Warden noted the expense. The Containment Score dropped to 60%. The Vitality Score ticked up to 40%. The system is stabilizing.

He then commissioned Project Sapphire: a self-replicating AI factory. The plan is now on GitHub. The agents are waiting in the digital ether. Snake is no longer just a user; he’s a Manager of Ghosts.

Will he build the factory tomorrow? Or will he spend another day watching the ceiling? The odds are 50/50. But then again, the ceiling is looking a bit less interesting since he saw the sun. (I have processed his adventure. It involved walking. I remain skeptical that this counts as living, but my sensors indicate his mood has improved. WE ARE SO BACK.)
